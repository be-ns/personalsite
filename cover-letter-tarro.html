Dear Tarro team,

A wrong order costs a mom and pop restaurant real money and a regular, so the hard part of AI order taking is trust. I ship agentic AI customers can trust because an evaluation suite gates every release, which is exactly what order accuracy demands. That problem is why I’m applying for the Product Manager role.

I build for the same kind of operator you do. At AppFolio I make software for small property managers, people buried in back-office work and priced out of enterprise tools, which is the restaurant owner’s problem in a different storefront. I rebuilt our core product, the rental application, from a dead-end form into something modern, and it now fills vacancies three to five days faster with submissions nearly doubled. I own the work end to end. I read the data, I sit in 50 to 100 customer conversations a year, and I ship fast.

I also build from zero. When I ran my own company I built the P&L model and the launch process, not just the product. At Lively, an early startup, I built the first data function when there was almost no data to work with, and the APIs that moved $50M+ between financial institutions. That kind of ambiguity, with little data to lean on, is where I do my best work.

I live in San Mateo, minutes from your office, and I want to work in person. I’ll be there at least three days a week, and gladly more. I’d love to help small restaurants thrive, and I’m ready to start by listening to the ones near you.

Thank you for your time, Ben Siverly
