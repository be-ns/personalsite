Dear Box Workflows hiring team,

I build software that fits how people actually work and quietly automates the parts they dread. That’s the whole job in your posting—turning advanced AI into business automation people trust—and it’s what I’ve spent the last few years doing. I’m applying for the Senior Product Manager, Workflows role with no inside connection, just conviction that this is work I’m built for.

At AppFolio I rebuilt a core workflow—the rental application—from a dead-end form into a modern, account-based product wired into our proprietary AI stack. Vacancies now fill three to five days faster and submissions have nearly doubled. What I care about most is how we got there: I start from the customer’s real job-to-be-done—I’m in 50 to 100 customer conversations a year, which is where the roadmap actually gets written—model the domain as objects an agent can act on, then gate every agentic release behind a behavioral spec and an evaluation suite. “Champion evaluation frameworks that build trust in AI-driven automation” is almost word-for-word the discipline I already practice, because an agent people don’t trust is an agent nobody adopts.

I also know what it takes to make integration real. At Lively I built the APIs and migration pipelines that moved $50M+ across financial institutions with zero data loss, and I authored the data model and patterns partners used to evaluate and onboard us. Turning a complex system into something a customer’s team will buy into and adopt is the outbound muscle this role needs—one I first built as a solo founder doing my own positioning, launch, and customer acquisition, and have used with every partner and stakeholder since.

I’ll be candid about the stretch: I’ve owned the product and lived in the customer’s world, but I haven’t yet owned the formal sales-enablement and deal-support surface end to end. That’s precisely the part I’m hungriest to take on—and where a founder’s instinct for positioning, narrative, and adoption is itching to be put to full use. I’m AI-first by default (I ship public tools with agentic, spec-driven development on the side), I’m in the Bay Area and glad to be in Redwood City, and I’d love to help make Box Automate the workflow layer enterprises actually rely on.

Thank you for reading, Ben Siverly
